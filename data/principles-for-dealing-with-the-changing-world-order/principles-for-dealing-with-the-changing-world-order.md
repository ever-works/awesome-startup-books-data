## Overview
A three-book collection for entrepreneurs and business leaders that covers:
- How successful startups are built and scaled (via case studies of HBS-founded companies)
- How to build and lead a data‑driven organization
- How to manage every stage of a startup journey from idea to execution

### Smart Startups (Hardcover)
- **Eighteen startup case studies** from companies founded by Harvard Business School graduates.
- **Contrasts with conventional wisdom** that entrepreneurs are simply “born geniuses” or follow a single, linear, billion‑dollar playbook.
- **Identifies common patterns** in:
  - Opportunity identification  
  - Early decision-making  
  - Scaling strategies  
  - Founder pitfalls and failures
- **Extracts “surprising lessons”** that differ from popular startup myths.
- **Highlights “unexpected pitfalls”** essential for aspiring founders to understand and avoid.

### Data Means Business (Paperback)
- **Explains why data‑driven decisions matter** for building resilient, adaptable organizations.  
- **Provides a proven framework** for leaders to:
  - Develop a **data‑guided mindset**  
  - Design **data-informed strategies**  
  - Generate **tangible business value from data**  
  - **Scale** data practices with organizational growth
- **Focus on culture-building**: How to embed a pragmatic data culture across teams.
- **Jargon-free writing**: Emphasizes clarity and accessibility for non‑technical leaders.
- **Packed with practical advice** rather than abstract theory.

### Managing Business Start-Ups (Paperback)
- **Covers the full entrepreneurial journey**, applicable whether you are:
  - Just starting out  
  - Already running an early‑stage or growing startup
- **Expert strategies and insights** to handle key stages and challenges.
- **From ideation to execution**:
  - Turning ideas into **validated concepts**  
  - Emphasis on **thorough market research** before scaling
- **Execution-oriented**: Designed to help move from concept to operating business.
