# Crossing the Chasm Workbook

**Category:** Marketing & Sales  
**Slug:** crossing-the-chasm-workbook

Companion workbook to Geoffrey Moore’s *Crossing the Chasm*, designed to help technology startups and teams working on disruptive products turn the book’s concepts into actionable go‑to‑market plans through structured exercises and templates.

Source: <https://geoffreyamoore.com/book/crossing-the-chasm/>

## Features

- **Action-oriented companion to *Crossing the Chasm***  
  Helps readers move from theory to practice, applying the core ideas of the book to their own startup or product.

- **Focus on disruptive innovations**  
  Tailored to startups and products that are introducing disruptive technologies into existing markets.

- **Market development guidance**  
  Structures work around the shift from selling to early adopters to winning over pragmatist mainstream customers.

- **Early adopter vs. pragmatist focus**  
  - Clarifies differences between visionary early adopters (who buy into a future vision) and pragmatists (who require proof and references).  
  - Encourages framing use cases in terms pragmatist customers recognize and trust.

- **Segment-focused go-to-market planning**  
  Guides teams to move away from broad, evangelistic outreach and instead:  
  - Target a **single, specific market segment**  
  - Focus on a **single, high-value, difficult use case** in one industry  
  - Build a playbook to “nail” that use case before expanding.

- **Target market selection exercises**  
  Provides structure for:  
  - Evaluating candidate segments  
  - Choosing a beachhead segment  
  - Defining segment boundaries and ideal customer profile.

- **Compelling reason to buy definition**  
  Helps teams identify and articulate:  
  - Concrete problems or pains driving purchase decisions  
  - Urgency and business impact for target customers.

- **Whole product planning**  
  Prompts users to map out the “whole product” needed for pragmatist adoption, including:  
  - Core product  
  - Essential integrations, services, and complements  
  - Onboarding and success elements required to deliver the promised result.

- **Partner and ally mapping**  
  Supports identifying and prioritizing:  
  - Partners needed to complete the whole product  
  - Allies and ecosystem players who influence the target segment.

- **Sales and marketing alignment**  
  Encourages alignment of:  
  - Messaging used by marketing with  
  - Sales processes, qualification, and deal strategy for the chosen segment.

- **Competitive positioning framework**  
  Helps position the company and product against incumbent competitors to become the clear leader in a defined niche.

- **Reflections and templates**  
  Includes structured reflections, worksheets, and templates (implied by it being a “workbook”) to document decisions and plans.

- **Designed for technology startups and product teams**  
  Especially relevant for founders, product leaders, and go-to-market teams navigating early market to mainstream transition.
