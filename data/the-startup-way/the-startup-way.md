# The Startup Way

**Type:** Book  
**Author:** Eric Ries  
**Category:** Startup Methodologies  
**Official site:** https://www.thestartupway.com/

## Summary
“The Startup Way” is Eric Ries’s follow-up to *The Lean Startup*. It explains how to apply lean startup principles inside scaling startups and large, established organizations to build systems for continuous innovation, entrepreneurial management, and experimentation that support long-term growth.

## Features
- **Entrepreneurial management for modern companies**  
  - Shows how modern organizations can use startup-style management practices.  
  - Focuses on transforming company culture to support ongoing innovation and learning.

- **Extension of Lean Startup principles**  
  - Adapts core Lean Startup ideas (experimentation, validated learning, fast feedback loops) to larger, more complex organizations.  
  - Addresses challenges specific to established companies as well as scaling startups.

- **Systems for continuous innovation**  
  - Describes how leaders can build repeatable processes for testing ideas, measuring impact, and scaling what works.  
  - Emphasizes long-term growth through continuous improvement rather than one-off initiatives.

- **Guidance for where organizations should begin**  
  - Provides direction on how companies can get started with entrepreneurial practices.  
  - Connects to related training, coaching, and consulting resources via the Lean Startup community (leanstartup.co).

- **Clarification of “managing entrepreneurially”**  
  - Explores what it really means to lead and manage like an entrepreneur inside a larger organization.  
  - Highlights the mindset and structures needed to preserve “startup DNA” as companies grow.
