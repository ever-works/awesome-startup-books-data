## Overview

Stanford professors Robert Sutton and Huggy Rao reveal how to replicate pockets of excellence across growing organizations without diluting quality.

## The Core Challenge

How do you grow from 100 to 1,000 to 10,000 without losing what made you special at 10?

## Scaling Principles

### 1. Spread a Mindset, Not a Footprint
- Focus on beliefs and behaviors
- Not just processes and metrics
- Culture compounds

### 2. I Own the Problem
- Accountability at every level
- No passing the buck
- Ownership scales, excuses don't

### 3. Cut Cognitive Load
- Simplify, simplify, simplify
- Reduce decisions people must make
- Standardize the routine

### 4. The People Who Fit
- Hire for culture and values
- Fire fast when misaligned
- One bad apple spoils the barrel

## Buddhism vs. Catholicism

**Catholic approach** (standardization):
- Strict rules and procedures
- Consistency through process
- Works for operational excellence

**Buddhist approach** (principles):
- Core values, flexible execution
- Adapt to local context
- Works for innovation

Most need both.

## Bad Seeds Destroy Faster Than Good Seeds Grow

One toxic person damages more than one great person helps. Guard the gates.

## Scaling Killers

- Broken mental models from founders
- Adding too fast (dilution)
- Tolerance for mediocrity
- Losing the core mindset
- Bureaucracy creep

## For Founders

- Define excellence clearly before scaling
- Fire fast when values don't align
- Simplify everything constantly
- Buddhism + Catholicism in balance
- Protect culture obsessively
