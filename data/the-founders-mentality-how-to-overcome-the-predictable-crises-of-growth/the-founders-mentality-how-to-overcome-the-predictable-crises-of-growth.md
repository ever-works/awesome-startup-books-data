## Overview

Bain consultants Chris Zook and James Allen studied why most companies lose their edge as they grow—and how to maintain founder's mentality at scale.

## The Three Elements of Founder's Mentality

### 1. Insurgent Mission
- Clear sense of the change you're creating
- Us vs. them mentality
- Energy from attacking the status quo

### 2. Owner's Mindset
- Think and act like owners
- Obsessed with details
- Hands-on problem solving
- Frugality and resourcefulness

### 3. Frontline Obsession
- Stay close to customers
- Fast decision-making
- Minimal bureaucracy
- Empower those closest to work

## Three Predictable Crises of Growth

### Crisis of Overload (Early Growth)
- Founder can't do everything anymore
- Need to delegate and systematize
- Maintain hands-on mentality

### Crisis of Stall-Out (Mature Growth)
- Complexity kills speed
- Bureaucracy creeps in
- Distance from frontline
- Solution: Reignite insurgency

### Crisis of Free Fall (Decline)
- Lost connection to core
- Chasing growth everywhere
- Solution: Return to founder's mentality or die

## Maintaining Founder's Mentality

- Hire for owner's mindset
- Keep insurgent mission alive
- Fight bureaucracy constantly
- Stay close to customers
- Celebrate frontline heroes
- Keep teams small

## Warning Signs

- Decisions take longer
- Distance from customers growing
- Layers multiplying
- Politics emerging
- Innovation slowing

## For Founders

- Name your insurgent mission
- Hire owners, not employees
- Fight bureaucracy from day one
- Never lose frontline connection
- Scale the mentality, not just the org
