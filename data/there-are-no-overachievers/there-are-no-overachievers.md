## Description
“There Are No Overachievers” is a personal development and productivity book that challenges traditional notions of success and performance. It focuses on how mindset and resilience shape long‑term achievement and wellbeing, especially for people operating under constant pressure—such as startup founders and other high performers. The book aims to help readers redefine success in a way that reduces burnout risk while supporting sustained, meaningful productivity.

## Features
- **Mindset-focused framework**
  - Encourages readers to rethink labels like “overachiever” and “underachiever.”
  - Emphasizes growth, learning, and self-awareness over external metrics and comparisons.

- **Resilience and sustainability**
  - Addresses the emotional and psychological toll of high expectations.
  - Offers approaches for maintaining performance over the long term instead of relying on short bursts of overwork.

- **Burnout and stress awareness**
  - Explores how constant striving and perfectionism can lead to exhaustion.
  - Helps readers identify patterns that increase burnout risk and adjust accordingly.

- **Redefining success**
  - Promotes a definition of success that includes wellbeing, relationships, and long-term impact.
  - Encourages aligning goals with personal values rather than purely external validation.

- **Relevance for founders and high performers**
  - Tailored to people managing intense workloads, ambitious goals, and pressure from investors, teams, or peers.
  - Offers perspective on how to stay effective without sacrificing health or motivation.

- **Productivity with purpose**
  - Focuses on doing the “right” work, not just more work.
  - Connects productivity strategies to mindset and emotional resilience.

## Ideal For
- Startup founders and entrepreneurs dealing with high expectations and long hours.
- Professionals in demanding, high-pressure roles.
- Readers interested in mindset, performance psychology, and personal growth.
- Anyone who has been labeled an “overachiever” or feels trapped by that identity.
