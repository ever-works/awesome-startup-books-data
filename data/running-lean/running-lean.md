### Overview
Running Lean is a practical guide for startup founders and product teams on how to move from an initial idea to a validated business model. It applies Lean Startup principles with a strong focus on hypothesis-driven experimentation, structured customer interviews, and actionable metrics, aiming to reduce risk and build products customers genuinely need.

### Features
- **Lean Startup foundation** – Translates Lean Startup concepts into step-by-step practices for early-stage ventures.
- **Systematic business model testing** – Guides readers in evolving from an initial “Plan A” to a validated plan that works through continuous iteration.
- **Hypothesis-driven approach** – Encourages framing all aspects of the business model (problem, solution, channels, revenue, etc.) as hypotheses to be tested.
- **Customer interviews & discovery** – Emphasizes direct conversations with customers to validate problems, refine solutions, and reduce guesswork.
- **Metrics and learning loops** – Focuses on selecting and tracking meaningful metrics to measure progress, validate learning, and inform pivot or persevere decisions.
- **Risk reduction** – Provides a structured way to de‑risk a startup by testing assumptions early before large investments in product development or marketing.
- **Product–market fit focus** – Orients all activities around converging on a business model that customers actually want and are willing to pay for.
