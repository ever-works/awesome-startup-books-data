## Overview

Jonah Berger's \"Contagious: Why Things Catch On\" distills years of research into understanding why certain ads, products, YouTube videos, political movements, songs, and restaurants catch on, while others are ignored.

## The STEPPS Framework

Berger developed six principles into an acronym STEPPS, which stands for:

### 1. Social Currency
The things we say affect how people see us. The more something makes someone look good, the more likely they'll be to pass it on.

### 2. Triggers
If something is top-of-mind it will be tip-of-the-tongue. Triggers stimulate people to think and talk about your product, idea, or service.

### 3. Emotion
When we care, we share. Whether positive (excitement or humor) or negative (anger or anxiety), high arousal emotions drive us to share.

### 4. Public
Making things more observable increases social influence. When we can see what others are doing, we're more likely to imitate it.

### 5. Practical Value
People share useful, valuable information. Highlighting incredible value drives word of mouth.

### 6. Stories
Information travels through narrative. People don't just share information, they tell stories.

## Word of Mouth Effectiveness

Word of mouth drives 20 to 50 percent of all purchasing decisions. Interestingly, only 7% of word-of-mouth content is shared online, with the vast majority happening through offline, face-to-face interactions.

## Key Insights for Startups

There's a science behind word of mouth. It's not random and it's not luck why people talk about some things rather than others. Regardless of how plain or boring a product or idea may seem, there are ways to make it contagious if you apply the right principles.

## Author Background

Jonah Berger is a marketing professor at the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania. His research has been published in top-tier academic journals and popular press outlets like The New York Times, Wall Street Journal, and Harvard Business Review.

## Impact

The book provides a set of specific, actionable techniques for helping information spread—for designing messages, advertisements, and information that people will share.
