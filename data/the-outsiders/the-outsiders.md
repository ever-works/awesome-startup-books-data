## Overview

The Outsiders: Eight Unconventional CEOs and Their Radically Rational Blueprint for Success by William N. Thorndike Jr. examines how eight little-known CEOs achieved extraordinary returns by thinking differently about capital allocation. These companies outperformed the S&P 500 by a factor of 20.

## About the Author

William N. Thorndike Jr. is the founder and managing partner of Housatonic Partners, a private equity firm. He graduated from Harvard College and Stanford Business School.

## The Central Insight

**Capital allocation is the CEO's most important job.**

Most CEOs focus on operations, strategy, and people. The outsider CEOs profiled focused intensely on how to deploy capital for maximum returns.

## The Eight Outsider CEOs

### 1. Tom Murphy - Capital Cities Broadcasting
- Bought ABC for $3.5 billion
- Decentralized operations
- Obsessive cost control
- Strategic acquisitions
- Returns: 19.9% annually (vs. 10.1% for S&P 500)

### 2. Henry Singleton - Teledyne
- Aggressive share buybacks when undervalued
- Issued stock when overvalued
- Conglomerate that actually worked
- Returns: 20.4% annually over 25 years

### 3. Bill Anders - General Dynamics
- Radical shrinkage strategy
- Exited unprofitable businesses
- Massive share buybacks
- Returned capital to shareholders
- Returns: 23.3% annually

### 4. John Malone - TCI (Tele-Communications Inc.)
- Maximized cash flow over earnings
- Minimal taxes through strategic debt
- Cable industry consolidation
- Returns: 30.3% annually over 25 years

### 5. Katharine Graham - The Washington Post
- Overcame gender barriers
- Hired talented managers (Buffett advised)
- Share buybacks and strategic acquisitions
- Returns: 18.9% annually

### 6. Warren Buffett - Berkshire Hathaway
- Float from insurance to fund investments
- Long-term value investing
- Acquired great businesses
- Returns: 20.7% annually over 40+ years

### 7. Dick Smith - General Cinema
- Diversification into bottling and retail
- Strategic timing of investments
- Thoughtful capital allocation
- Returns: 16.1% annually

### 8. Bill Stiritz - Ralston Purina
- Focused on cereals and pet food
- Strategic divestitures
- Share buybacks
- Returns: 20.0% annually

## Common Traits

### Independent Thinking
- Ignored conventional wisdom
- Made contrarian decisions
- Didn't follow industry norms
- Thought from first principles

### Focus on Cash Flow
- Emphasized cash over accounting earnings
- Understood economic reality vs. GAAP
- Managed for cash generation
- Used cash wisely

### Disciplined Capital Allocation
The five key options for deploying capital:
1. Invest in existing operations
2. Acquire other businesses
3. Pay dividends
4. Pay down debt
5. Buy back stock

Outsider CEOs excelled at choosing the best option.

### Decentralized Operations
- Small corporate headquarters
- Empowered business unit leaders
- Minimal bureaucracy
- Focus on results, not process

### Frugality
- Low overhead
- Modest compensation
- No corporate jets or perks
- Every dollar counts

### Long-Term Orientation
- Patient capital deployment
- Willing to wait for opportunities
- Ignored quarterly earnings pressure
- Multi-year perspective

### Opportunistic
- Acted boldly when opportunities arose
- Built cash for deployment
- Countercyclical investing
- Bought when others panicked

## Capital Allocation Lessons

### Share Buybacks
When to buy back stock:
- Stock trading below intrinsic value
- Better return than alternatives
- Excess cash available
- Creates shareholder value

Singleton at Teledyne bought back 90% of shares over time.

### Strategic Acquisitions
Criteria for acquisitions:
- Attractive prices
- Good businesses
- Minimal integration needed
- Cash flow positive

Avoid overpaying in auctions.

### Divestitures
Willing to:
- Exit underperforming businesses
- Sell at peak valuations
- Simplify and focus
- Return capital to shareholders

Anders at General Dynamics exited most businesses.

### Using Stock as Currency
Issue stock when:
- Stock is overvalued
- Can buy assets cheaper than building
- Creates shareholder value

Singleton issued stock in bubble, bought back in crashes.

## Why These CEOs Were Overlooked

- Didn't seek publicity
- Operated outside major financial centers
- Unconventional strategies
- Focused on results, not PR
- Let performance speak for itself

## Comparison to "Professional" CEOs

Typical CEO focus:
- Growing revenue and earnings
- Expanding operations
- Building empires
- Following industry norms

Outsider CEO focus:
- Maximizing per-share value
- Allocating capital optimally
- Shrinking when appropriate
- Thinking independently

## Applications for Founders

### Capital Allocation Framework
- Evaluate all uses of capital
- Choose highest return options
- Be patient and opportunistic
- Don't default to conventional choices

### Building vs. Buying
- Sometimes buying is cheaper
- Don't let ego drive build decisions
- Acquisitions can create value
- But avoid overpaying

### When to Return Capital
- If you can't deploy it well
- When stock is undervalued (buybacks)
- To maintain flexibility
- Shareholders can allocate too

### Culture of Frugality
- Every dollar matters
- Low overhead enables flexibility
- Model discipline from the top
- Question all expenses

## Why It Matters for Startups

While focused on public company CEOs, lessons apply to:
- How to think about fundraising
- Discipline in deploying capital
- Focus on cash flow and unit economics
- Independent thinking vs. following trends
- Long-term value creation
