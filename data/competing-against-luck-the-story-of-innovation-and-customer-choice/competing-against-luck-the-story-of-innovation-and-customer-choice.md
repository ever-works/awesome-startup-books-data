## Overview

From the legendary author of The Innovator's Dilemma comes Competing Against Luck, explaining how to understand what causes customers to buy products and services. The Jobs-to-be-Done theory shows that customers don't buy products—they hire them to make progress in their lives.

## The Jobs Theory

Customers hire products to do jobs:
- **Functional job**: The core task to be done
- **Emotional job**: How they want to feel
- **Social job**: How they want to be perceived

## Key Concepts

- Jobs are stable, even as solutions change
- Circumstances matter more than customer characteristics
- Non-consumption is often the biggest competitor
- Hire and fire: customers hire solutions and fire unsatisfactory ones

## Real-World Examples

- **Milkshake Marketing**: McDonald's discovered people hire milkshakes for different jobs (morning commute vs. treat for kids)
- **IKEA**: People hire IKEA to "furnish my first apartment quickly and cheaply"
- **Airbnb**: Hired to "feel at home while traveling" not just "find lodging"

## For Founders

- Define the job your product is hired to do
- Understand competing solutions (including non-consumption)
- Design experiences around the job, not just features
- Create products customers can't help but hire
