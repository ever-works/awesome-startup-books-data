## Founder vs Investor

**Category:** Biographies  
**Brand/Publisher:** BenBella Books  
**Slug:** `founder-vs-investor`

### Overview
“Founder vs Investor” is a candid book on startups and venture capital that explains how fundraising really works from seed stage through IPO. It focuses on the incentives, term sheets, and negotiation dynamics that shape the often-misunderstood relationship between founders and venture capital investors. It is aimed primarily at startup founders who want to raise capital intelligently and navigate the full growth journey of a venture-backed company.

### Features
- **Fundraising lifecycle coverage**  
  - Explains fundraising from early seed rounds through later stages and IPO.  
  - Describes how capital needs and investor expectations evolve over time.

- **VC and founder incentives**  
  - Details the economic and strategic incentives driving venture capital firms.  
  - Explores how these incentives may align or conflict with founder goals.  
  - Helps founders anticipate investor behavior in different market conditions.

- **Term sheet explanations**  
  - Breaks down common term sheet components.  
  - Clarifies rights, preferences, and obligations often embedded in investment documents.  
  - Aims to demystify legal and financial jargon so founders can evaluate offers more clearly.

- **Negotiation dynamics**  
  - Describes typical negotiation scenarios between founders and investors.  
  - Highlights power dynamics at different stages (e.g., competitive rounds vs. down rounds).  
  - Provides insight into how both parties think about valuation, control, and risk.

- **Founder–VC relationship**  
  - Focuses on the ongoing relationship after a deal closes, not just on the transaction.  
  - Addresses board interactions, expectations, and governance.  
  - Discusses common misunderstandings and friction points between founders and investors.

- **Practical guidance for founders**  
  - Targets startup founders raising or planning to raise venture capital.  
  - Emphasizes raising capital “intelligently,” with awareness of long-term implications.  
  - Orients readers to the realities of building and scaling a venture-backed company.
