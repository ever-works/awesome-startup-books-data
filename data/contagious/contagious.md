## Overview

Contagious: Why Things Catch On is a New York Times bestseller by Wharton marketing professor Jonah Berger that explains the science behind why products and ideas become popular through word of mouth.

## About the Author

Jonah Berger is a Marketing Professor at the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania and a world-renowned expert on word of mouth, viral marketing, social influence, and how products catch on. He was a student of Chip Heath at Stanford.

## The STEPPS Framework

Berger identifies six principles that drive products to go viral:

### 1. Social Currency
- People share things that make them look good
- Create insider knowledge
- Leverage game mechanics
- Make people feel like insiders

### 2. Triggers
- Top of mind means tip of tongue
- Create mental associations
- Design environmental reminders
- Frequent triggers beat novel ones

### 3. Emotion
- When we care, we share
- High-arousal emotions drive sharing
- Awe, excitement, humor, anger work
- Sadness doesn't drive sharing

### 4. Public
- Built to show, built to grow
- Make the private public
- Behavioral residue
- Observable products spread faster

### 5. Practical Value
- News you can use
- Help people help others
- Highlight incredible value
- Frame deals effectively

### 6. Stories
- Information travels under the guise of idle chatter
- Build a Trojan Horse
- Make your message integral to the narrative
- Valuable virality

## Key Insights

- Word of mouth is more effective than advertising
- Only 7% of word of mouth happens online
- The messenger matters as much as the message
- Virality isn't luck—it can be engineered
- Remarkable isn't enough; you need the right triggers

## Applications for Startups

- Design products that are naturally sharable
- Create triggers that remind people of your product
- Build social currency into your offering
- Craft stories that carry your message
- Make usage public and visible
- Provide practical value worth sharing

## Why It Matters for Founders

Most startups rely on word of mouth for growth but don't understand how to generate it systematically. Contagious provides a science-backed framework for making products and ideas spread.
