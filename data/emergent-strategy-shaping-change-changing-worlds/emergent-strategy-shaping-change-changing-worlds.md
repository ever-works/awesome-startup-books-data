# Emergent Strategy: Shaping Change, Changing Worlds

- **Type:** Book
- **Author:** adrienne maree brown  
- **Publisher / Brand:** AK Press  
- **Category:** Leadership & Management  
- **Tags:** leadership, change, strategy  
- **Source:** https://www.akpress.org/emergentstrategy.html

## Overview
“Emergent Strategy: Shaping Change, Changing Worlds” outlines a framework for adaptive, relationship-centered leadership and social change. While written for organizers and movement-builders, its ideas are broadly applicable to startup founders and teams operating in uncertain, rapidly changing environments.

## Features
- **Emergent strategy framework** focused on adapting to change rather than controlling it.
- **Relationship-centered leadership** that prioritizes trust, connection, and interdependence over rigid hierarchies.
- **Guiding principles**, including:
  - *“Small is good”* – emphasizing the power of small-scale experiments and incremental change.
  - *“Change is constant, be like water”* – encouraging flexibility and responsiveness in dynamic conditions.
  - *“Move at the speed of trust”* – pacing work and growth according to the strength of relationships.
  - *“Never a failure, always a lesson”* – treating experiments and setbacks as learning opportunities.
- **Application to social change and organizing**, offering tools and mindsets for groups working toward transformative justice.
- **Relevance to startups and innovation**, especially for navigating uncertainty, experimentation, and scaling impact in complex systems.

## Use Cases
- Founders and startup teams seeking frameworks for experimentation and learning.
- Leaders and managers aiming to build trust-centered, adaptive organizations.
- Community organizers and activists designing strategies for long-term social change.
- Facilitators, coaches, and consultants looking for new ways to approach complexity and collaboration.
