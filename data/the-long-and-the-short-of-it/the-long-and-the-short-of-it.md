## Overview

“The Long and the Short of It” is a founders-focused personal finance and investing primer. It explains how financial markets, investment products, and risk work in clear, plain language to help startup founders make better decisions about:

- Managing their own capital and liquidity
- Handling current and future equity proceeds
- Building and preserving long‑term wealth outside their company

## Features

- **Founders-centric perspective**
  - Tailored to startup founders, especially those dealing with illiquid equity and uncertain income.
  - Focuses on decisions before and after liquidity events (e.g., acquisitions, IPOs, secondary sales).

- **Plain‑language explanations**
  - Breaks down how financial markets function.
  - Clarifies common investment products (e.g., stocks, funds, other vehicles) without jargon.
  - Explains key risk concepts so non‑finance readers can apply them.

- **Personal finance fundamentals for founders**
  - Helps readers think about diversification beyond their startup equity.
  - Encourages long‑term wealth planning instead of concentrating everything in a single company.

- **Investing and risk education**
  - Introduces how risk and return trade‑offs work.
  - Discusses how different investment products behave in various market conditions.

- **Application to real founder situations**
  - Focus on using the concepts for:
    - Managing cash after funding rounds or exits.
    - Deciding when and how much equity exposure to keep.
    - Structuring long‑term portfolios outside the startup.
