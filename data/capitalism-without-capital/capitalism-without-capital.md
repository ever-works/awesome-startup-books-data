## Capitalism Without Capital: Book Summary

**Title:** Capitalism Without Capital

**Authors:** Jonathan Haskel and Stian Westlake

**Category:** Growth & Scaling

**Tags:** Intangible Assets, Growth, Innovation

**Description:**
"Capitalism Without Capital" is a groundbreaking exploration of the modern economy's shift towards intangible assets, such as design, branding, and intellectual property. The authors, Jonathan Haskel and Stian Westlake, delve into how these intangible investments are shaping startups and driving economic growth and innovation.

**Key Insights:**
- **Intangible Assets Dominance:** The book highlights a shift in economic investment, where intangible assets have surpassed tangible assets in many leading economies, including the US and EU nations.
- **Role of Technology:** Although technological advancements like computers and the internet have increased the value of intangible investments, the trend predates these innovations.
- **Policy and Intangibles:** Certain policy environments, such as increased public R&D spending and reduced market regulation, correlate with higher levels of intangible investment.

**Features:**
- **Economic Analysis:** Provides a comprehensive analysis of how intangible assets are influencing economic growth and the rise of tech-driven companies.
- **Historical Context:** Traces the evolution of intangible investments from the mid-20th century to the present.
- **Global Perspective:** Examines trends in both developed and developing countries, providing a broad understanding of the intangible economy.
