## Overview

The Infinite Game is a 2019 book by Simon Sinek, applying ideas from James P. Carse's similarly titled book to topics of business and leadership. The book is both a New York Times and Wall Street Journal bestseller.

## Core Concept: Finite vs. Infinite Games

In finite games, like football or chess, the players are known, the rules are fixed, and the endpoint is clear, with winners and losers easily identified. In infinite games, like business or politics or life itself, the players come and go, the rules are changeable, and there is no defined endpoint.

Sinek argues that many organizations approach their goals with a finite mindset, aiming to win in a specific time frame and beat the competition, while an infinite mindset involves a long-term perspective, focusing on ongoing adaptation and improvement.

## Impact on Organizations

Groups that adopt an infinite mindset enjoy vastly higher levels of trust, cooperation and innovation and all the subsequent benefits. Leaders who embrace an infinite mindset will build stronger, more innovative, inspiring, resilient organizations, though these benefits may accrue over larger timescales.

## Five Essential Practices

Sinek outlines five interrelated practices that enable leaders to adopt an infinite mindset:

1. **A Just Cause** - A specific vision of a future state that inspires sacrifice
2. **Trusting Teams** - An environment where people feel psychologically safe
3. **Worthy Rivals** - Competitors who reveal our weaknesses and push us to improve
4. **Existential Flexibility** - The courage to make profound strategic shifts
5. **The Courage to Lead** - The willingness to lead with vulnerability

These practices are not sequential steps but ongoing disciplines that help organizations remain durable, morally grounded, and adaptive.

## Key Insights

The search for success and value based on finite goals undermines the very element that is essential to success: trust. Organizations that embrace an infinite mindset prioritize purpose over profit and sustainable value creation over quarterly earnings.
