- **Category:** Biographies
- **Format:** Audiobook
- **Source:** [Apple Books – Shoe Dog (Unabridged)](https://books.apple.com/us/audiobook/shoe-dog-unabridged/id1439481739)
- **Tags:** entrepreneurial-journey, global-brand, startup-life

## Description
Shoe Dog is Nike founder Phil Knight’s memoir, focusing on the early, scrappy years of building Nike from a small startup into a global athletic brand. Knight offers a candid account of the messy, risky, and often chaotic path of entrepreneurship, highlighting the struggles, setbacks, and persistence required to grow a company over decades.

## Features
- **Founding story of Nike**
  - Begins with Phil Knight, fresh out of business school, borrowing $50 from his father.
  - Initial mission: import high-quality, low-cost running shoes from Japan.
  - Early sales made from the trunk of Knight’s car (starting in 1963).
  - First-year revenue of about $8,000, contrasted with Nike’s later multi‑billion‑dollar scale.

- **Entrepreneurial journey and startup life**
  - Details Knight’s decision to build something independent rather than join a large corporation.
  - Explores the risks taken at each stage of growth.
  - Describes “messy, perilous, and chaotic” phases of building a business over many years.

- **Challenges and obstacles**
  - Accounts of crushing setbacks faced by the company in its formative years.
  - Encounters with ruthless competitors.
  - Tense relationships and conflicts with hostile bankers and financial pressures.

- **Growth into a global brand**
  - Traces Nike’s evolution from an intrepid startup to one of the world’s most recognized and influential sports brands.
  - Shows how the Nike “swoosh” became an instantly recognizable global icon.

- **Key relationships and company culture**
  - Highlights Knight’s partnership with his former track coach, Bill Bowerman.
  - Introduces early employees portrayed as a "ragtag group of misfits and savants."
  - Depicts how these early team members formed a close-knit, mission-driven culture (“swoosh‑crazed brothers”).

- **Themes and focus areas**
  - The transformative power of sports and its role in shaping Nike’s vision.
  - The importance of shared belief and bold vision in building a company.
  - Honest reflection on mistakes, sacrifices, and learning along the way.

- **Narrative style (audiobook)**
  - Memoir-style storytelling that reads like a narrative rather than a business manual.
  - Per Apple’s editorial description, the performance (by Norbert Leo Butz) presents the story with dramatic, novel-like pacing.
