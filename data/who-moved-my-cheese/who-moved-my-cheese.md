## Overview

Who Moved My Cheese? is a 1998 motivational business fable by Spencer Johnson that describes four reactions to change. The book uses a simple parable to explore profound truths about dealing with change in both professional and personal spheres.

## Commercial Success

As of 2018, it has sold almost 30 million copies worldwide in 37 languages and remains one of the best-selling business books. The book spent over 200 weeks on Publishers Weekly's hardcover nonfiction list.

## Core Message

The book follows four characters—two mice (Sniff and Scurry) and two "littlepeople" (Hem and Haw)—as they navigate a maze in search of cheese, which serves as a metaphor for what we desire in life.

## The Four Characters

The story features characters representing different reactions to change:

- **Sniff** is a mouse who quickly detects change and moves forward
- **Scurry** is a mouse who acts fast when change happens
- **Hem** is a little person who fears change and resists it
- **Haw** is a little person who initially hesitates but learns to embrace change

## Business Application

The book has been used by wide-ranging companies as a tool for managing organizational change, winning praise for its ability to foster resilience and adaptability among employees. It provides practical wisdom for dealing with change in work and life.

## Key Lessons

The fable teaches that change is inevitable, and those who anticipate, adapt to, and enjoy change will be more successful than those who resist it.
