### Overview
“The Art of War” is a classic work on strategy and tactics, attributed to Sun Tzu. It is frequently recommended to startup founders and business leaders for its timeless lessons on competition, positioning, leadership, and strategic thinking in high-stakes or uncertain environments.

### Features
- **Core focus on strategy:** Principles for planning and executing strategy in complex, competitive contexts.
- **Competition and positioning:** Guidance on understanding rivals, choosing where and how to compete, and positioning for advantage.
- **Leadership insights:** Emphasis on the role of the leader in decision‑making, morale, discipline, and clear communication.
- **Use of intelligence and information:** Stresses the value of preparation, research, and understanding conditions before acting.
- **Adaptability:** Encourages flexibility, responding to changing circumstances rather than rigidly following plans.
- **Efficient use of resources:** Advocates winning with minimal waste and avoiding unnecessary conflict.
- **Risk management:** Focus on choosing battles wisely and avoiding direct confrontation when it is not advantageous.
- **Relevance to startups and business:** Often applied to startup strategy, market entry, competitive differentiation, and team leadership in high‑stakes business environments.
