## Overview

“Lean Impact” adapts the principles of *The Lean Startup* to social impact ventures and mission-driven organizations. It is designed for founders, nonprofit leaders, and innovators who are tackling large social and environmental problems and need a structured way to test, refine, and scale their solutions.

## Features

- **Lean innovation framework for impact**  
  Applies lean startup methods (build–measure–learn cycles, rapid iteration, validated learning) to nonprofit and social enterprise contexts.

- **Focus on large-scale social and environmental challenges**  
  Tailored to teams working on complex issues such as poverty, health, education, climate, and other mission-driven areas.

- **Practical experimentation tools**  
  Provides step-by-step approaches for running experiments, testing assumptions, and validating solutions in real-world social settings.

- **Impact-oriented metrics and validation**  
  Emphasizes measuring social and environmental outcomes, not just financial or usage metrics, and using data to refine programs and services.

- **Guidance on scaling impact**  
  Addresses how to move from small pilots to large-scale implementation while maintaining effectiveness and learning.

- **Case studies and real-world examples**  
  Includes examples from social ventures and mission-driven organizations to illustrate how lean methods are applied in practice.

- **Frameworks for founders and leaders**  
  Offers models and structures to help leaders make decisions about program design, resource allocation, and strategic pivots in pursuit of greater impact.

## Ideal For

- Social entrepreneurs and founders of impact-driven startups  
- Nonprofit and NGO leaders  
- Program and innovation teams in mission-driven organizations  
- Anyone looking to systematically test and improve solutions to social or environmental problems
