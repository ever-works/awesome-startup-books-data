## Overview

Sprint is a New York Times bestseller by Jake Knapp and former Google Ventures partners that presents a unique five-day process for solving tough problems, proven at more than a hundred companies.

## The Core Concept

The Design Sprint was created at Google by Jake Knapp. The big idea is to build a prototype of your idea and test it with real customers. This method is like fast-forwarding into the future, so you can see how customers react before you invest all the time and expense of creating your new product, service, or campaign.

## The Five-Day Process

In a Design Sprint, you take a small team, clear your schedules for a week, and rapidly progress from problem, to prototype, to tested solution using the step-by-step five-day process:

- **Monday**: Map out the problem and pick an important place to focus
- **Tuesday**: Sketch competing solutions on paper
- **Wednesday**: Make difficult decisions and turn your ideas into a testable hypothesis
- **Thursday**: Build a high-fidelity prototype
- **Friday**: Test it with real target customers

## History and Authors

Originally created by Jake Knapp at Google in 2010, the Design Sprint has since been adopted by thousands of teams around the world. Jake Knapp is co-founder and general partner at Character Capital and a New York Times bestselling author who previously helped build Gmail and Microsoft Encarta, cofounded Google Meet, and was a partner at Google Ventures.

The book was co-authored with John Zeratsky and Braden Kowitz, both also former partners at Google Ventures.

## Impact

The Sprint methodology has been used by companies like Slack, LEGO, Blue Bottle Coffee, and the United Nations to solve critical business challenges quickly and effectively.
