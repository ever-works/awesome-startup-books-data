## Good Strategy Bad Strategy

**Category:** Business Strategy  
**Brand:** Amazon  
**Author:** Richard Rumelt  
**Type:** Book

### Overview
Good Strategy Bad Strategy explains what distinguishes effective strategy from vague aspiration. It provides clear concepts and tools for analyzing challenges, making hard choices, and aligning actions. While written for a broad business audience, it is widely recommended to startup founders and leaders as a foundational guide to strategic thinking and competitive advantage.

### Features
- **Explains what “good strategy” is**
  - Defines good strategy as a coherent response to a critical challenge.
  - Emphasizes focus, clear choices, and coordinated action.

- **Diagnoses “bad strategy” patterns**
  - Shows how wishful thinking, fluff, and broad goals substitute for real strategy.
  - Helps readers recognize empty slogans and unfocused initiatives.

- **Strategy diagnosis frameworks**
  - Provides methods for identifying the core challenge in a competitive or organizational situation.
  - Encourages looking past surface symptoms to underlying structure and constraints.

- **Guidance on making strategic choices**
  - Stresses the need to prioritize and concentrate resources on a few key objectives.
  - Highlights trade-offs and the importance of saying “no” to non-essential efforts.

- **Action alignment and execution focus**
  - Links high-level strategy to concrete, coordinated actions across the organization.
  - Shows how to turn a central insight into a “coherent action” plan.

- **Applicable beyond large enterprises**
  - Though not startup-specific, its principles apply to early-stage companies, product strategy, and competitive positioning.
  - Frequently recommended to startup founders as a must-read on strategic thinking.

- **Focus on competitive advantage**
  - Examines how to identify, build, and leverage sources of advantage.
  - Encourages realistic assessment of strengths, weaknesses, and market dynamics.

### Use Cases
- Founders clarifying company or product strategy.
- Leaders distinguishing real strategy from vision or goal-setting.
- Teams aligning initiatives and roadmaps around a small number of key challenges.
