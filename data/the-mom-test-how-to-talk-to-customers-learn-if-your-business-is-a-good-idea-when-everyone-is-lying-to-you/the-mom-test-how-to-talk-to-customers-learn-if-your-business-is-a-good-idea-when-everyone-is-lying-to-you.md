## Overview

The Mom Test is about how to talk to customers and learn if your business is a good idea when everyone is lying to you at least a little. It's a short, practical book that shows you how to get honest feedback through better questions.

## The Core Rule

Talk about their life instead of your idea. Ask about specifics in the past instead of generics or opinions about the future.

## Key Principles

- Don't ask if they would use/buy—ask about past behavior
- Don't ask what they want—ask what they've tried
- Don't pitch—listen for problems
- Don't ask leading questions—ask open-ended ones
- Compliments are worthless—look for commitment

## Good vs Bad Questions

**Bad**: "Would you use an app that helps you organize recipes?"
**Good**: "Tell me about the last time you tried to find a specific recipe."

**Bad**: "Is price an issue for you?"
**Good**: "What's your budget for this type of solution?"

## The Three Rules

1. **Talk about their life, not your idea**
2. **Ask about specifics in the past, not generics or opinions about the future**
3. **Talk less, listen more**

## What to Look For

- Currency: time, reputation risk, or money
- If they haven't paid in one of these currencies, they aren't serious
- The best indicator is what they've already done, not what they say they'll do
