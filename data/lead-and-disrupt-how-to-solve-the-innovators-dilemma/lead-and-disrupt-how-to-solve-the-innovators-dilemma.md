## Lead and Disrupt: How to Solve the Innovator’s Dilemma

**Type:** Book  
**Authors:** Charles A. O’Reilly & Michael L. Tushman  
**Category:** Disruption & Innovation  
**Brand/Publisher:** Stanford Business Books – Stanford University Press

### Overview
“Lead and Disrupt” is a management and innovation strategy book that addresses how organizations can resolve the classic innovator’s dilemma: sustaining current performance while building the next wave of growth. The authors introduce a practical framework for running an existing core business and, at the same time, pursuing new, potentially disruptive opportunities.

### Key Concept
- **Dual-Engine Model**  
  - **Engine 1 – Exploit:** Focus on executing and optimizing the existing business, improving efficiency, and delivering reliable performance.  
  - **Engine 2 – Explore:** Pursue new growth opportunities, disruptive innovations, and experimental business models, even when they challenge or cannibalize Engine 1.

### Features
- Explains how to **simultaneously exploit and explore** within the same organization.
- Provides a structured lens for **balancing core execution with continuous innovation**.
- Addresses the **organizational and leadership challenges** of managing two different operating modes (execution vs experimentation).
- Offers guidance highly relevant for **startup founders and leaders** who must protect and grow their core while placing bold, disruptive bets.
- Connects to the broader **“innovator’s dilemma”** problem and proposes a practical resolution through ambidextrous leadership and strategy.

### Use Cases
- Founders designing an org structure that supports both **operational excellence** and **innovation teams**.
- Leaders in scaling companies who need to **protect the core business** while investing in **new products, markets, or technologies**.
- Strategy and innovation practitioners seeking a **framework for ambidextrous organizations**.
