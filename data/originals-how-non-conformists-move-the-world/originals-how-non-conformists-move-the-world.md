## Overview

Originals is a groundbreaking book that shows how to recognize a good idea, speak up without getting silenced, build a coalition of allies, choose the right time to act, and manage fear and doubt. Adam Grant addresses the challenge of improving the world from the perspective of becoming original: choosing to champion novel ideas and values that go against the grain, battle conformity, and buck outdated traditions.

## Key Insights

- **The Myth of the Risk-Taker**: Successful originals are actually risk-averse in other areas of life
- **Procrastination Can Help**: Strategic procrastination can boost creativity
- **First-Mover Disadvantage**: Being first isn't always best; improving on others' ideas often wins
- **Quantity Breeds Quality**: More ideas increase the odds of true originality
- **Groupthink is the Enemy**: How to build cultures where dissent is valued
- **The Power of Doubt**: Self-doubt can motivate excellence

## What Founders Will Learn

- How to generate and recognize original ideas
- When to take risks and when to hedge your bets
- Building a culture where innovation thrives
- Timing your launch for maximum impact
- Managing fear and doubt productively
- How to persuade others to support new ideas
- The importance of diverse coalitions

## Practical Applications

- Techniques for voicing new ideas without career risk
- How to identify which ideas are worth pursuing
- Methods for building support for unconventional proposals
- Strategies for overcoming resistance to change
