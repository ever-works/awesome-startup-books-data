## Overview

**The Entrepreneurs** is a historical and analytical book by Derek Lidow that traces entrepreneurs across cultures and millennia. It examines how the persistent drive to create and capture value has repeatedly reshaped economies and societies, offering a deep, long-term perspective on entrepreneurship that extends beyond contemporary Silicon Valley narratives. It is especially relevant for startup founders seeking context on how entrepreneurial behavior has evolved over time.

## Features

- **Long-run historical scope** – Covers entrepreneurs across multiple cultures and thousands of years.
- **Cross-cultural analysis** – Examines similarities and differences in entrepreneurial activity in different societies.
- **Focus on value creation and capture** – Analyzes how entrepreneurs generate and appropriate value, and the impact of those activities.
- **Economic and social impact** – Explores how entrepreneurs have transformed economies and influenced social structures throughout history.
- **Analytical framework** – Provides conceptual tools to understand entrepreneurship as a recurring force, not just a modern phenomenon.
- **Context beyond Silicon Valley** – Places modern startups within a much broader historical and global narrative.
- **Relevance for founders** – Offers insights for startup founders who want to ground their practice in long-term patterns of entrepreneurial behavior.
