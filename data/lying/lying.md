## Overview
“Lying” is a philosophical and practical examination of honesty. It argues for radically reducing or eliminating lies in personal and professional life, and is recommended for startup founders as part of a YC-endorsed reading list.

## Features
- **Focus on honesty and integrity**  
  Explores why telling the truth (or saying nothing) is almost always preferable to lying.

- **Framework for founders**  
  Provides a lens for startup founders to think about truthfulness in relationships with co‑founders, employees, customers, and investors.

- **Culture-building guidance**  
  Offers ideas for building a company culture rooted in trust, integrity, and clear, direct communication.

- **Practical decision-making**  
  Encourages founders to examine "white lies," omissions, and spin, and to consider long‑term effects on credibility and team morale.

- **Ethical perspective**  
  Discusses the moral and philosophical implications of lying vs. radical honesty in modern life and business.

- **Communication clarity**  
  Emphasizes transparent communication as a foundation for effective collaboration and stakeholder alignment.

## Ideal For
- Startup founders and leaders
- Early-stage teams setting company values
- Anyone interested in the ethics and practice of honesty in professional contexts
