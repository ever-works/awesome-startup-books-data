## Overview

Poke the Box is Seth Godin's manifesto about the initiative you're taking—or not taking—in your job and your life. It's about prodding the system, making things happen, and being the person who starts things.

## Core Message

**Just start.**

The cost of being wrong is less than the cost of doing nothing. The world rewards those who ship, who initiate, who make things happen.

## Key Principles

### Starting Is Everything
- Initiative beats perfection
- Action trumps planning
- Starting > Being right
- Shipping > Preparing

### The Initiator
The person who:
- Starts projects
- Ships products
- Makes connections
- Takes responsibility
- Accepts risk

### Permission
You don't need permission to:
- Start something
- Make art
- Take initiative
- Ship your work
- Make a difference

## Why We Don't Start

- Fear of failure
- Waiting for permission
- Seeking perfection
- Analysis paralysis
- Cultural conditioning to follow

## The Poking Philosophy

Poke: Take action, make a move, start something
- Test boundaries
- Try new approaches
- Learn by doing
- Iterate quickly

## For Founders

Startups are built by people who poke the box:
- Launch before you're ready
- Ship minimum viable products
- Test and learn
- Fail forward
- Keep starting

The greatest risk is not starting at all.

## Connection to Other Godin Works

Expands on themes from:
- Linchpin (being indispensable)
- Purple Cow (being remarkable)
- The Dip (knowing when to quit)

Focuses specifically on initiative and starting.
