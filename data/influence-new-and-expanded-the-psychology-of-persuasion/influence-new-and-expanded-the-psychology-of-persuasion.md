## Overview

Cialdini identifies seven universal principles of persuasion backed by decades of research. Understanding these helps you ethically influence and recognize manipulation.

## The Seven Principles

### 1. Reciprocation
People feel obligated to return favors. Give value first.

### 2. Commitment and Consistency
People want to be consistent with past actions. Get small yeses first.

### 3. Social Proof
People follow what others do. Show popularity and testimonials.

### 4. Liking
People say yes to those they like. Build genuine connections.

### 5. Authority
People defer to experts. Demonstrate credibility.

### 6. Scarcity
People want what's limited. Create genuine scarcity.

### 7. Unity (New)
People say yes to "us". Build shared identity.

## Applications

- **Sales**: Social proof, scarcity, reciprocation
- **Marketing**: Authority, liking, consistency
- **Fundraising**: Unity, authority, social proof
- **Hiring**: Liking, reciprocation, commitment

## Ethical Use

Cialdini emphasizes these work best when authentic. Manipulation backfires.

## For Founders

- Understand why people say yes
- Recognize when you're being influenced
- Apply ethically in business
- Design products with these principles
