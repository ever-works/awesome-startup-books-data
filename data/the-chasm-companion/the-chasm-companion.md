## Overview

**The Chasm Companion** is a practical field guide designed to extend Geoffrey A. Moore’s classic books **"Crossing the Chasm"** and **"Inside the Tornado."** It focuses on helping startups and high‑growth companies apply the chasm framework to real-world go‑to‑market and product decisions.

---

## Features

- **Practical extension of chasm theory**
  - Builds on concepts from "Crossing the Chasm" and "Inside the Tornado"
  - Translates theory into actionable guidance for operators

- **Field-guide format**
  - Structured as a hands-on manual rather than a purely conceptual book
  - Oriented toward day-to-day use by founders, product leaders, and GTM teams

- **Tools and templates**
  - Provides concrete tools to diagnose where a product is on the technology adoption lifecycle
  - Offers structured approaches for segmenting markets and selecting a beachhead segment

- **Checklists for execution**
  - Step-by-step checklists to plan and execute a chasm-crossing strategy
  - Helps teams align product, marketing, and sales activities around a target segment

- **Real-world examples**
  - Illustrates chasm concepts with applied examples from startups and technology companies
  - Shows how different go‑to‑market choices affect adoption across segments

- **Go‑to‑market decision support**
  - Guidance on positioning, messaging, and target customer profiles for early markets vs. mainstream markets
  - Helps evaluate which channels, pricing, and packaging are appropriate at different lifecycle stages

- **Product strategy alignment**
  - Connects product roadmap choices to chasm-stage realities
  - Emphasizes whole-product thinking to meet mainstream customer expectations

- **For startups and growth-stage companies**
  - Written specifically for founders, early employees, and GTM leaders
  - Applicable across B2B and B2C technology markets where adoption patterns follow the chasm model

## Ideal Readers

- Startup founders and co-founders
- Product managers and product leaders
- Marketing and go‑to‑market leaders in tech companies
