## Overview

New Sales. Simplified. is a 12-year bestseller with over 2,000 five-star Amazon reviews. It removes the mystery from prospecting and provides a proven formula for finding, developing, and closing deals. No gimmicks, no manipulation—just fundamental sales skills that work.

## The Core Message

**Sales is simple, but not easy.** Success comes from doing the fundamentals consistently:
- Target the right prospects
- Make effective contact
- Tell a compelling story
- Ask for the business

## Key Principles

- **Prospecting is Non-Negotiable**: If you don't have prospects, you don't have a business
- **Activity Drives Results**: You can't manage outcomes, but you can manage activity
- **Your Story Matters**: Lead with business value, not product features
- **Target Selection is Strategic**: Focus on accounts you can win and that are worth winning
- **Phone is Not Dead**: Combined with email and social, it's your most powerful tool

## The New Sales Simplified Framework

### 1. Selecting Targets
- Create a strategic list of target accounts
- Focus on prospects who need what you sell and can afford it
- Resist the temptation to chase everyone
- Quality over quantity in target selection

### 2. Creating Weapons
- Sales story: Why should prospects care about you?
- Phone voicemail script that gets callbacks
- Prospecting emails that get opened and responded to
- LinkedIn messaging that starts conversations
- Referral request scripts

### 3. Planning the Attack
- Monthly planning: set activity goals
- Weekly planning: schedule prospecting blocks
- Daily execution: protect your prospecting time
- Use a CRM to track everything

### 4. Making Contact
- Phone + email + social = the trifecta
- The "3x3" contact cadence
- Leaving voicemails that differentiate you
- Email subject lines that get opened
- LinkedIn techniques that feel natural, not salesy

### 5. Conducting Sales Calls
- Discovery: understand their business and pain
- Presentation: lead with business value, not features
- Handling objections without being defensive
- Trial closes throughout the conversation

### 6. Advancing and Closing
- Always leave with a next step
- Ask for the business (most salespeople don't)
- Handle price objections confidently
- Know when to walk away

## The "Sales Story"

Your sales story should answer:
- Who are we and why should you care?
- What business issues do we address?
- What results do clients get from working with us?
- Who specifically have we helped and how?

Lead with business outcomes, not your product's features.

## On Prospecting Activity

Weinberg is blunt: Most salespeople fail because they don't prospect enough. He provides specific activity targets:
- Proactive prospecting time: 40-50% of your week
- New conversations started per week: specific targets
- Proposals sent per month: track it
- Pipeline coverage: 3-4x your quota

## Common Mistakes Addressed

- Chasing unqualified prospects
- Talking about your product instead of their problems
- Not asking for the business
- Giving up after one or two attempts
- Letting inbound leads distract from proactive prospecting
- Over-relying on social media instead of picking up the phone
